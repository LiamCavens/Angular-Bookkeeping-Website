Transcription

use a human transcriber?

While there are many electronic programmes now on offer which enable the transcription of the spoken word, they have yet to master the art of human interpretation. While a machine might be capable of interpreting the words of a single, non-accented speaker enunciating clearly into a good quality recording apparatus, with no background noise, no interruptions and no jargon, in reality, life situations don`t quite happen like that!


Verbatim

This type of transcription includes all the "uhms" "ers" and non vocabulary actions and gestures which make up, and are as much of a part of our language, as the spoken word. Good transcripts should be rich with these and as a transcriber, it takes a keen ear to identify and transcribe these accurately.
